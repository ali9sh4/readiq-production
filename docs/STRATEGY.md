# Rubik Strategy — Strategic Objective & Operating Priorities

Last updated: 2026-07-23 · Owner: Ali · Review: quarterly · Hard gate: end of 2027

## 1. The Number (Primary Aim)
- Exit: sell Rubik for $8–12M by 2031 (stretch: 2030). Max 1–2 years earn-out after sale.
- Requires ~$2.5–3M annual profit at exit (3–4× multiple).
- GMV ladder: $5K/mo (end 2026) → $25K/mo (2027) → $100K/mo (2028) → $300K/mo (2029) → $600K/mo (2030–31).
- Interim non-negotiable: $5K/month owner profit by end of 2027 (the "life number").
- Re-forecast gate (end 2027): if GMV < ~$20K/mo, timeline extends to 7–8 years. Recalibration, not failure. A $2–3M exit in year 5 still transforms life; $10M is the stretch target that disciplines strategy.

## 2. The Opportunity
Arabic-speaking professionals — beachhead: Iraqi dentists — lack trusted, practical, career-advancing education. Experts lack a safe, profitable way to monetize knowledge (piracy fear, no production capacity, no payment rails). Rubik solves both sides.

## 3. The Business We Are In
Career outcomes, not courses.
- EN: Rubik turns expertise into opportunity — income for those who teach, advancement for those who learn.
- AR: روبيك يحوّل الخبرة إلى فرصة — دخل لمن يعلّم، وتقدّم لمن يتعلّم.
Every feature (AI questions, exams, certificates, future job market) serves this one promise. Features that do not serve it get cut.

## 4. Prototype Standard (Gerber franchise test)
Rubik is "done" when one launch runs end-to-end — recruit → deal → film → approve → launch → exam → certificate → payout — with Ali supervising, not doing.
- Operations Manual to be written as docs over time: instructor pipeline, launch playbook, student lifecycle, money procedures.
- Geographic ladder: Iraqi dental → Iraqi medical/health → Arabic-speaking region.
- Buyer profile: Gulf edtech / regional platform / telecom. Implications: clean books from day one, Arabic-first content, completed company registration, standardized deal terms (end bespoke revenue splits; standard is 70/30).

## 5. Non-negotiables
Instructor-approved content only · honest certificates · protected IP · immutable financial ledgers.

## 6. Current State & Bottleneck (2026-07)
- 7 courses live. Platform + apps feature-complete. Only feature allowed to ship: exam + certificate (already promised in recruitment messaging).
- Traction: ~$1,500 gross in first launch period, zero marketing. (Enrollment count vs revenue discrepancy unresolved — verify.)
- Price architecture gap: only $30–70 self-study tier exists. A flagship/cohort tier ($200–400) is required to reach the life number.
- THE BOTTLENECK IS INSTRUCTOR ACQUISITION — not tech, not content capacity (4–5 courses/month possible). Ali's role for the next 6 months: Chief Instructor Recruiter.
- Mitigations: Rubik-owned courses bypass the bottleneck (100% margin, Ali can teach); one visibly successful instructor transforms all future negotiations.

## 7. Operating Priorities (order matters)
1. Prove the machine (30–60 days): one real launch of the best-selling course. Ship exam + certificate on it first. 2–3 weeks Instagram clips + one free live session → paid push. Outputs: revenue number, testimonials, case study.
2. Instructor pipeline (parallel): 5–10 named targets who have audiences. Discovery conversations (Mom Test style), close later with launch proof. Filter: real expertise AND audience/willingness to promote. No "anyone" recruiting.
3. Rubik-owned flagship: cohort program, ~$300 × 20 seats ≈ $6K per launch. Ali teaches v1.
4. Demand engine: IG clips → partnerships (colleges, النقابة, dental supply companies via course-packages feature) → referral mechanics. Paid ads only after organic proves the message.
5. Everything else waits: AI agent ≤ 20% of time; new verticals opportunistic-only; feature backlog frozen.
